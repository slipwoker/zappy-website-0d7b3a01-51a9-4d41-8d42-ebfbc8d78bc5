How to think about fit.

Proper device fit is essential for patient safety and procedural success. Always consult with qualified healthcare professionals and follow manufacturer instructions carefully. Sizing and placement should be determined by trained medical personnel based on patient anatomy and clinical assessment.

Sizing overview

Device sizing is determined by patient anatomical measurements and clinical indicators. Healthcare providers should reference sizing charts and consider factors such as age, weight, and anatomical variations. Proper measurement techniques and assessment protocols ensure optimal device selection for each patient.

Comfort and placement notes

Correct placement requires careful attention to anatomical landmarks and patient positioning. Medical professionals should verify placement through established clinical protocols and monitoring. Patient comfort indicators and procedural feedback help confirm appropriate fit and positioning throughout use.
